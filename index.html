How popular is Afrobeats, really?

Even though several worldstars have dabbled in the genre, some still consider it "niche".

By Rebecca Habtemariam

This year the grammys introduced a new award category: "Best African Performance". The nominies were Asake, Arya Starr, Burna Boy, Davido and Tyla. Even though it was Tyla, the only Non-Afrobeats artist in this category, who won the award, the introduction of this new category signaled to many people: Afrobeats has officially entered the mainstream.

This thesis can be questioned, though. In many established western pop culture critique spaces, like newspapers or television shows, Afrobeats still seems to be considered as a niche genre and therefore doesn't get a lot of coverage.

The Merriam Webster Dictionary defines the adjective "mainstream" as " having, reflecting, or being compatible with the prevailing attitudes and values of a society or group". In this article a genre "being compatible with" a society of group will be interpreted as something that a lot of people are listening to. To determine if Afrobeats is mainstream, three parameters of music consumption will be analized:

  • Playlists
  • YouTube Subsciptions
  • Festival Lineups

First, we look at three popular Afrobeats-affiliated Spotify playlists and their development during the last five years.

This is a fairly new released playlist. Even though it already reached almost 90.000 Followers on Spotify (as of 13th July 2024), there isn't a clear trend visible due to being recently published.

The Afropop Playlist had a slow start, but is continiously growing since four years. Over 400.000 people follow this playlist.

This is by far the most successfull playlist. In the last five years, the follower count of "African Heat" has grown over a 1000 percent.

But where do all these followers come from? Is the genre only popular within a certain region or is it a geographically widespread success? Since YouTube is a platform that gets used by most countries around the globe, I checked the subscription data of some of most popular Afrobeats artists (according to Spotify). The map below shows the "Top countries" that YouTube (via Chartmetrics) has assigned for each of those artists, together with their subscribtion number.

The last two graphics shows that Afrobeats not only keeps rising in popularity, but has millions of fans all over the world. Therefore, it is mainstream.

But next to media outlets, which I mentioned in the beginning, there are many other gatekeepers in the music industry that can influence an artist's or a genre's growth. One of them are festival appearances. In the next few graphics you'll see the appearance of Afrobeats artists in three big, "mixed-genre" festivals: Glastonbury, Coachella and the German Version of Lollapalooza. Their line-ups get compared to last.fm's list of people that they have tagged under "Afrobeats". To see, if the rising popularity of the last few years is also visible in the festival line-ups, I compare the line-ups of the festivals in 2022 and 2024.

Glastonbury Line-Up 2022 (left) vs. 2024 (right)

Glastonbury has a few Afrobeats artists in both line-up's, but they actually were less this year than two years earlier.

Coachella Line-Up 2022 vs. 2024

Not only the total number of artists is way smaller, but also the number of Afrobeats artists. But in relation to the total number, this years Coachella line-up had a stronger Afrobeats presence than Glastonbury 24. Two years ago though, the genre didn't play any role in the valley.

Lollapalooza Berlin Line-Up 2022 vs. 2024

After Burna Boy sold out the London O2 arena twice, he now celebrates his Berlin Lollapalooza debut. If the festival will include more Afrobeats artists in the future - especially since many of the genre's fans reside in Germany - remains to be seen.

This data shows clearly that Afrobeats is far from being niche and has in fact a worldwide growing fanbase. Even though not even all Afrobeats artists are totally happy with this development, like a recent speech from Rema showed, the popularity of the genre can not be denied. Therefore, culture makers, influencers and critics should give this genre the time and effort it deserves.
